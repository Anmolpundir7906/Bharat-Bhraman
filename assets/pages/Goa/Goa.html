1→ Goa

Introduction to Goa

Goa, renowned for its pristine beaches, vibrant culture, and lively nightlife, is also home to several underrated and offbeat places waiting to be explored. Away from the bustling tourist hotspots, these hidden gems offer a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and tranquility. Let's embark on a journey to uncover four such lesser-known destinations in Goa.

Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary

About the Place: Tucked away in the southern part of Goa, Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is a hidden gem known for its dense forests, diverse wildlife, and tranquil ambiance. Spread over an area of 86 square kilometers, the sanctuary is home to several species of flora and fauna, including giant squirrels, langurs, and Malabar pit vipers. Cotigao is also renowned for its scenic beauty, with winding trails, cascading waterfalls, and picturesque viewpoints offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys.

Geography

Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the Canacona taluka of South Goa, approximately 76 kilometers from the state capital of Panaji. Situated amidst the Western Ghats, the sanctuary is characterized by its lush evergreen forests, rolling hills, and meandering streams, creating a haven for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers.

How to Reach

From Panaji, Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary can be reached by road via NH 66. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Canacona Railway Station, the nearest railway station to the sanctuary, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the entrance.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the wildlife is more active. However, travelers should be prepared for occasional showers, as the sanctuary receives rainfall throughout the year. The monsoon season (June to September) offers lush greenery and cascading waterfalls, making it an ideal time for photography and birdwatching.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary offers limited accommodation options, including forest rest houses and eco-lodges, where visitors can stay amidst the wilderness. For budget travelers, there are also homestays and guesthouses available near the sanctuary entrance. As for food, visitors are advised to carry their own provisions, as there are no restaurants or eateries within the sanctuary premises.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its diverse wildlife, Cotigao Wildlife Sanctuary is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Cotigao Wildlife Interpretation Center, where visitors can learn about the sanctuary's ecology and conservation efforts, and the Canopy Walkway, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and valleys. Visitors can also embark on guided nature walks and birdwatching tours to explore the sanctuary's hidden gems, including its endemic plant species and rare birdlife.

2→ Chorao Island

Introduction to Chorao Island

About the Place: Nestled in the Mandovi River, Chorao Island is a serene oasis known for its lush mangrove forests, tranquil backwaters, and rich avian diversity. The island is home to the Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary, named after the renowned Indian ornithologist, which is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. Chorao is also renowned for its historic churches, ancient temples, and traditional Goan villages, offering visitors a glimpse into the island's rich cultural heritage.

Geography

Chorao Island is located in North Goa, approximately 15 kilometers from the state capital of Panaji. Situated amidst the Mandovi River, the island is characterized by its dense mangrove forests, meandering waterways, and serene ambiance, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and birdwatchers

How to Reach

From Panaji, Chorao Island can be reached by road via NH 748. The journey takes approximately 30-40 minutes by car or bus, including a short ferry ride across the Mandovi River. Alternatively, one can take a ferry from Ribandar Ferry Terminal to Chorao Island, which operates at regular intervals throughout the day.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Chorao Island is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the birdlife is more active. However, travelers can also visit during the monsoon season (June to September) to witness the lush greenery and blooming flowers, although birdwatching may be limited due to heavy rainfall.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Chorao Island offers limited accommodation options, including guesthouses and homestays, where visitors can stay amidst the natural surroundings. For those seeking a more luxurious experience, there are also resorts and boutique hotels available in nearby Panaji. As for food, visitors can explore local eateries and restaurants serving traditional Goan cuisine, including seafood dishes like fish curry, prawn balchao, and crab xacuti, along with vegetarian specialties like mushroom xacuti and poi.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its bird sanctuary, Chorao Island is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Our Lady of Grace Church, a historic church dating back to the Portuguese colonial era, and the Shree Mahalaxmi Temple, dedicated to the Hindu goddess of wealth and prosperity. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Divar Island, known for its ancient churches and scenic beauty, and the Dr. Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary, offering guided birdwatching tours and boat cruises.

3→ Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary

Introduction to Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary

4→ Divar Island
