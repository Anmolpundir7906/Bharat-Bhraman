1→ Kinnaur

Introduction to Kinnaur

Kinnaur is the most beautiful valley and sightseeing location in Himachal Pradesh.It is also the less travelled location because of its very far distance and also at high altitude. It is also known as The Land of Apples and Gods. Kinnaur Valley is one of the most beautiful valleys and one of the 12 districts of Himachal Pradesh. It is renowned for apples and orchards, lovely meadows covered with dense woods and the most adventurous routes that cut through these valleys for a mesmerizing road trip. While before you get excited of its highly magnificent Valleys and meadows, Kinnaur districtt is also known for following, preaching, and preserving the ancient Himachali culture, where you get to worship to the ancient temples and greet you with the delightful Himachali food also.You will find the place in the form of interesting architecture, ancient temples and delicious local cuisines. So let's take you to one of the most beautiful valleys in Kinnaur districtt.

2→ Sangla Valley

Introduction to Sangla Valley

Located 71km from the main Kinnaur districtt, Sangla Valley in Himachal is probably one of the most beautiful mountain villages in India. Its breathtaking natural landscapes have made many travellers fall in love with the place. The valley is picturesque, especially during spring and autumn, when the flowers and apple orchards are in full bloom or when the leaves change their colour. You do not want to miss that. But like always, the main charm of a place is its people, their villages and cultural history. When in Sangla Valley, do explore what’s left of an old fort, beautiful temples built in traditional architecture, and the rich Tibetan culture of the people. Sangla valley id also nice for adventure as Adventure seekers can indulge in activities like trekking, camping, river rafting, angling, and mountain biking. Believe it or not, fishing for Himalayan trout can be a fun adventure if you haven't tried it yet. Apart from the bustling cities, the sangla valley offers a really peacefull and serene environment.

3→ Chitkul

Introduction to Chitkul

Chitkul is nothing short of a traveler’s paradise. This place could be an ideal choice for adventure lovers. Chitkul is located at an altitude of about 11,319 ft which makes it a bit difficult to reach. With Kinner Kailash in the backdrop, Chitkul Is unparalleled beauty on the banks of Baspa River. It is said to be the last village on the Hindustan-Tibet route and the first village of Baspa valley. On one side of the river, you can witness the beauty of the snow-clad mountains while the other side has a terrain full of wooden houses and apple orchards. For the most part of the year, the place remains covered under a thick layer of snow. BASPA RIVER - Baspa river is an important river, which flows all the way through the hills of Sangla. This place is considered as ideal for anglers as the river is home to a wide variety of fishes including the brown trout and the rainbow trout.
