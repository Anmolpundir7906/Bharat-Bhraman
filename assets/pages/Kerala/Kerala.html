1→ Kerala

Introduction to Kerala

Kerala, famously known as "God's Own Country," is renowned for its backwaters, lush greenery, and rich cultural heritage. While popular destinations like Munnar and Alleppey attract tourists in large numbers, there are several underrated and offbeat places in Kerala waiting to be discovered. Let's embark on a journey to uncover four such hidden gems, offering a glimpse into the state's lesser-known wonders.

2→ Vagamon

About the Place

Tucked away amidst the Western Ghats, Vagamon is a quaint hill station known for its rolling meadows, misty valleys, and tranquil ambiance. The town is blessed with breathtaking landscapes, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Vagamon is also home to several trekking trails, lush tea plantations, and picturesque viewpoints, offering panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Geography

Vagamon is located in the Idukki district of Kerala, approximately 100 kilometers from the state capital of Thiruvananthapuram. Situated at an altitude of 1,100 meters above sea level, the town enjoys a cool and pleasant climate throughout the year, making it a popular retreat from the sweltering heat of the plains.

How to Reach

From Thiruvananthapuram, Vagamon can be reached by road via NH 183. The journey takes approximately 3-4 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Kottayam, the nearest railway station to Vagamon, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the hill station.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Vagamon is during the post-monsoon season, from September to March, when the weather is pleasant, and the hills are covered with lush greenery. However, travelers should avoid visiting during the monsoon months (June to August) as heavy rains can lead to landslides and disrupt travel plans.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Vagamon offers a range of accommodation options, including budget guesthouses, homestays, and luxury resorts, where visitors can find comfortable lodging amidst the natural surroundings. For budget travelers, there are also dormitory-style accommodations available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and tea stalls serve a variety of Kerala cuisine, including appam, stew, and kappa biryani, along with traditional snacks like banana fritters and pazham pori.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its scenic beauty, Vagamon is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Vagamon Meadows, a vast expanse of grasslands offering breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys, and the Pine Forest, a serene woodland area ideal for nature walks and picnics. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Kurisumala Ashram, a spiritual retreat nestled amidst the hills, and the Murugan Mala, a sacred hill dedicated to Lord Murugan.

3→ Silent Valley National Park

About the Place

Hidden away in the Nilgiri Hills, Silent Valley National Park is a pristine wilderness area known for its rich biodiversity, dense rainforests, and untouched beauty. The park is home to several rare and endangered species of flora and fauna, including the lion-tailed macaque, Nilgiri langur, and Malabar giant squirrel, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Silent Valley is also renowned for its scenic beauty, with cascading waterfalls, meandering streams, and panoramic viewpoints dotting the landscape.

Geography

Silent Valley National Park is located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, approximately 90 kilometers from the city of Coimbatore in neighboring Tamil Nadu. The park is spread over an area of 237 square kilometers, encompassing tropical rainforests, grasslands, and montane forests, with the Kunthipuzha River flowing through its heart.

How to Reach

From Coimbatore, Silent Valley National Park can be reached by road via NH 544. The journey takes approximately 3-4 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Palakkad, the nearest railway station to the park, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the entrance.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Silent Valley National Park is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry, and the wildlife is more active. However, travelers should be prepared for occasional showers, as the park receives rainfall throughout the year. The monsoon season (June to September) offers lush greenery and cascading waterfalls, making it an ideal time for photography and birdwatching.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Silent Valley National Park offers limited accommodation options, including forest rest houses and eco-lodges, where visitors can stay amidst the wilderness. For those seeking a more luxurious experience, there are also resorts and homestays available near the park entrance. As for food, visitors are advised to carry their own provisions, as there are no restaurants or eateries within the park premises.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its pristine wilderness, Silent Valley National Park is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Sairandhri Visitor Center, where visitors can learn about the park's ecology and conservation efforts, and the Kuntipuzha River, ideal for birdwatching and wildlife spotting. Visitors can also embark on guided trekking expeditions to explore the park's hidden gems, including its diverse flora and fauna and scenic viewpoints.

4→ Nelliyampathy

About the Place

Perched atop the Western Ghats, Nelliyampathy is a scenic hill station known for its lush tea plantations, misty valleys, and panoramic views. The town is blessed with a cool and salubrious climate, making it an ideal destination for nature walks, trekking, and birdwatching. Nelliyampathy is also renowned for its orange orchards, cardamom plantations, and serene waterfalls, offering visitors a refreshing escape amidst the tranquility of the hills.

Geography

Nelliyampathy is located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, approximately 75 kilometers from the city of Palakkad. Situated at an altitude of 1,500 meters above sea level, the hill station offers panoramic views of the surrounding plains and forests, with the Nelliyampathy Range forming its backdrop.

How to Reach

From Palakkad, Nelliyampathy can be reached by road via NH 544. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Palakkad Railway Station and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the hill station.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Nelliyampathy is during the post-monsoon season, from September to February, when the weather is pleasant, and the hills are covered with lush greenery. However, travelers should avoid visiting during the monsoon months (June to August) as heavy rains can lead to landslides and disrupt travel plans.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Nelliyampathy offers a range of accommodation options, including budget guesthouses, homestays, and luxury resorts, where visitors can find comfortable lodging amidst the natural surroundings. For budget travelers, there are also dormitory-style accommodations available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and tea stalls serve a variety of Kerala cuisine, including puttu, kadala curry, and kappa biryani, along with traditional snacks like banana chips and achappam.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its scenic beauty, Nelliyampathy is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Palagapandi Estate, one of the oldest tea plantations in Kerala, and the Seethargundu Viewpoint, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and plains. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Pothundi Dam, a picturesque reservoir ideal for boating and picnics, and the Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, home to several rare and endangered species of flora and fauna.





5→ Bekal

About the Place

Nestled along the Arabian Sea coast, Bekal is a hidden gem known for its pristine beaches, historic forts, and tranquil backwaters. The town is home to the iconic Bekal Fort, a 17th-century fortress built by the Keladi Nayaka dynasty, offering panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the surrounding coastline. Bekal is also renowned for its serene backwaters, meandering rivers, and picturesque lagoons, making it an ideal destination for boat cruises and houseboat stays.

Geography

Bekal is located in the Kasaragod district of Kerala, approximately 70 kilometers from the city of Mangalore in neighboring Karnataka. Situated along the Malabar Coast, the town is blessed with a tropical climate, with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 35°C throughout the year.

How to Reach

From Mangalore, Bekal can be reached by road via NH 66. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Kasaragod Railway Station and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the town.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bekal is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is pleasant, and the sea is calm, making it ideal for beach activities and sightseeing. However, travelers should avoid visiting during the monsoon months (June to September) as heavy rains can disrupt travel plans and water-based activities.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Bekal offers a range of accommodation options, including beach resorts, guesthouses, and homestays, where visitors can find comfortable lodging with stunning views of the Arabian Sea. For those seeking a more luxurious experience, there are also luxury resorts and heritage hotels available near the fort. As for food, local eateries and beach shacks serve a variety of Kerala cuisine, including seafood dishes like fish curry, prawn fry, and karimeen pollichathu, along with traditional Malabar snacks like pathiri and meen mulakittathu.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its historic fort, Bekal is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Bekal Beach, a pristine stretch of coastline ideal for swimming and sunbathing, and the Bekal Hole Aqua Park, offering water sports activities like jet skiing, banana boating, and parasailing. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Chandragiri Fort, a 17th-century fortress overlooking the Chandragiri River, and the Valiyaparamba Backwaters, a serene network of lagoons and canals ideal for boat cruises and kayaking.
