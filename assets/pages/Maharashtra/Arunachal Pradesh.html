1→ Maharashtra

Introduction to Maharashtra

Maharashtra, known for its bustling cities, rich history, and stunning coastline, is also home to several underrated and offbeat destinations waiting to be explored. Away from the tourist crowds, these hidden gems offer a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and tranquility. Let's embark on a journey to discover four such lesser-known places in Maharashtra.

2→ Bhandardara

About the Place

Nestled in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, Bhandardara is a serene hill station known for its tranquil lakes, lush greenery, and breathtaking vistas. The town is home to the Arthur Lake, a picturesque reservoir formed by the Pravara River, surrounded by verdant hills and dense forests. Bhandardara is also renowned for its historical sites, including the Wilson Dam and the ancient Agasti Rishi Ashram.

Geography

Bhandardara is situated in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, approximately 185 kilometers from the state capital of Mumbai. The town is perched at an altitude of 750 meters above sea level, offering a cool and pleasant climate throughout the year.

How to Reach

From Mumbai, Bhandardara can be reached by road via NH 160. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Igatpuri, the nearest railway station to Bhandardara, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the town.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bhandardara is during the monsoon months, from June to September, when the region is at its greenest, and the waterfalls are in full flow. However, travelers should be prepared for heavy rainfall during this time. Alternatively, the post-monsoon season (October to February) offers pleasant weather and clear skies, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Bhandardara offers a range of accommodation options, including guesthouses, resorts, and homestays, where visitors can find comfortable lodging amidst the natural surroundings. For budget travelers, there are also dormitory-style accommodations available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and restaurants serve a variety of Maharashtrian cuisine, including vada pav, pav bhaji, and thali meals.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its scenic beauty, Bhandardara is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Randha Falls, a majestic waterfall located near the Wilson Dam, and the Amruteshwar Temple, an ancient Shiva temple known for its intricate carvings and architecture. Visitors can also embark on trekking expeditions to nearby peaks like Mount Kalsubai, the highest peak in Maharashtra.

3→ Malshej Ghat

About the Place

Perched amidst the rugged hills of the Western Ghats, Malshej Ghat is a scenic mountain pass known for its breathtaking landscapes, cascading waterfalls, and diverse flora and fauna. The region is home to several migratory bird species, including flamingos, herons, and cranes, making it a paradise for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. Malshej Ghat is also renowned for its ancient fortresses, including Harishchandragad and Shivneri, which offer panoramic views of the surrounding valleys.

Geography

Malshej Ghat is located in the Pune district of Maharashtra, approximately 150 kilometers from Mumbai. The mountain pass is situated at an altitude of 700 meters above sea level, offering panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the plains below.

How to Reach

From Mumbai, Malshej Ghat can be reached by road via NH 222. The journey takes approximately 3-4 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Kalyan, the nearest railway station to Malshej Ghat, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the mountain pass.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Malshej Ghat is during the monsoon months, from June to September, when the region is enveloped in mist and the waterfalls are in full flow. However, travelers should exercise caution while driving on the narrow mountain roads during this time. Alternatively, the post-monsoon season (October to February) offers pleasant weather and clear skies, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Malshej Ghat offers limited accommodation options, including guesthouses, resorts, and camping sites, where visitors can find comfortable lodging amidst the natural surroundings. For budget travelers, there are also dormitory-style accommodations available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and dhabas serve a variety of Maharashtrian and North Indian cuisine, including bhakri, pitla, and misal pav.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its scenic beauty, Malshej Ghat is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Pimpalgaon Joga Dam, a popular picnic spot known for its scenic views and boating facilities, and the Ajoba Hill Fort, a historic fortress located atop a hill offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys. Visitors can also trek to the nearby Harishchandragad Fort, known for its ancient temples and rock-cut caves.

4→ Tarkarli

About the Place

Tucked away on the Konkan coast of Maharashtra, Tarkarli is a hidden gem known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and coral reefs. The town is renowned for its water sports activities, including snorkeling, scuba diving, and parasailing, making it a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts and beach lovers. Tarkarli is also home to the historic Sindhudurg Fort, built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in the 17th century.

Geography

Tarkarli is situated in the Sindhudurg district of Maharashtra, approximately 500 kilometers from Mumbai. The town is nestled along the shores of the Arabian Sea, with its coastline dotted with palm-fringed beaches and fishing villages.

How to Reach

From Mumbai, Tarkarli can be reached by road via NH 66. The journey takes approximately 10-12 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Kudal, the nearest railway station to Tarkarli, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the town.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Tarkarli is from October to March when the weather is pleasant, and the sea is calm, making it ideal for water sports activities and beach outings. However, travelers should avoid visiting during the monsoon months (June to September) as heavy rains can disrupt travel plans and water sports activities.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Tarkarli offers a range of accommodation options, including beach resorts, guesthouses, and homestays, where visitors can find comfortable lodging with stunning views of the Arabian Sea. For budget travelers, there are also dormitory-style accommodations available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and beach shacks serve a variety of seafood dishes, including fresh fish, prawns, and crabs, as well as traditional Malvani cuisine.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its pristine beaches, Tarkarli is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Sindhudurg Fort, a historic sea fortress built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, and the Devbagh Beach, known for its tranquil ambiance and scenic beauty. Visitors can also take a boat ride to the nearby Tsunami Island, a small uninhabited island surrounded by crystal-clear waters, ideal for snorkeling and beach picnics.





5→ Kaas Plateau

About the Place

Known as the "Valley of Flowers of Maharashtra," Kaas Plateau is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning display of seasonal wildflowers and endemic plant species. Located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, the plateau transforms into a colorful carpet of flowers during the monsoon season, attracting nature lovers and photographers from far and wide. Kaas Plateau is also home to several rare and endangered species of flora and fauna, making it a biodiversity hotspot.

Geography

Kaas Plateau is situated approximately 280 kilometers from Mumbai, amidst the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra. The plateau is perched at an altitude of 1,200 meters above sea level, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

How to Reach

From Mumbai, Kaas Plateau can be reached by road via NH 48. The journey takes approximately 6-7 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Satara, the nearest railway station to Kaas Plateau, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the plateau.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kaas Plateau is during the monsoon months, from August to September, when the plateau is in full bloom with vibrant wildflowers and lush greenery. However, travelers should be prepared for heavy rainfall and slippery trails during this time. Alternatively, the post-monsoon season (October to November) offers clear skies and pleasant weather, making it ideal for exploring the plateau and enjoying the panoramic views.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Kaas Plateau offers limited accommodation options, including guesthouses and homestays, where visitors can find comfortable lodging amidst the natural surroundings. For budget travelers, there are also camping sites available at reasonable rates. As for food, local eateries and dhabas in nearby villages serve a variety of Maharashtrian cuisine, including bhakri, pithla, and sol kadhi.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from its stunning wildflowers, Kaas Plateau is home to several attractions worth exploring. These include the Kaas Lake, a serene water body surrounded by lush greenery and frequented by migratory birds, and the Thoseghar Waterfalls, one of the tallest waterfalls in Maharashtra, known for its cascading beauty. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Chalkewadi Windmill Farms and the Ajinkyatara Fort, offering panoramic views of the Western Ghats.
