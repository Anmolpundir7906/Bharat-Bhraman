1→ TELANGANA

Introduction to TELANGANA

Telangana, a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, is home to several underrated and offbeat destinations waiting to be explored. Away from the bustling city life, these hidden gems offer a unique blend of heritage, tranquility, and scenic beauty. Let's embark on a journey to uncover four such offbeat destinations in Telangana.

Bhongir Fort

About the Place: Bhongir Fort, also known as Bhuvanagiri Fort, is a majestic hilltop fortress steeped in history and architectural grandeur. Perched atop a monolithic rock hill, the fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding plains and countryside. Built during the 10th century by the Western Chalukya ruler Tribhuvanamalla Vikramaditya VI, the fort served as a strategic stronghold and played a significant role in the region's defense. Today, it stands as a testament to Telangana's rich heritage and serves as a popular trekking destination for adventure enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

Geography

Bhongir Fort is located in the Yadadri Bhuvanagiri district of Telangana, approximately 50 kilometers from the state capital of Hyderabad. Situated atop a massive rock hill, the fort overlooks the town of Bhongir and is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a picturesque destination for visitors.

How to Reach

From Hyderabad, Bhongir Fort can be reached by road via NH 163. The journey takes approximately 1-2 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Bhongir Railway Station, located at the foothills of the fort, and then hike or take a local auto-rickshaw to reach the entrance of the fort.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Bhongir Fort is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities like trekking and sightseeing. However, visitors should avoid visiting during the peak summer months (April to June) due to scorching temperatures.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Bhongir town offers limited accommodation options, including budget hotels and guesthouses, where visitors can find comfortable lodging at affordable rates. As for food, there are several eateries and roadside stalls in Bhongir town serving local delicacies and snacks. For a wider range of options, visitors can explore nearby towns like Yadagirigutta and Bibinagar.

Nearby Attractions

Apart from Bhongir Fort, there are several other attractions worth exploring in the vicinity. These include the Yadagirigutta Temple, a popular pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Narasimha, and the Panagal Reservoir, a scenic water body ideal for picnics and boating. Visitors can also explore the historic town of Aler, known for its ancient temples and architectural marvels.

2→ Ethipothala Falls

Introduction to Ethipothala Falls

About the Place: Ethipothala Falls is a hidden gem nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Nallamala forest range. Formed by the cascading waters of the Chandravanka River, the waterfall plunges from a height of approximately 70 feet, creating a mesmerizing spectacle. Surrounded by rocky cliffs and dense vegetation, Ethipothala Falls offers a tranquil retreat for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts. The area around the waterfall is also home to several species of birds and wildlife, making it an ideal spot for birdwatching and wildlife photography.

Geography

Ethipothala Falls is located near Nagarjuna Sagar in the Nalgonda district of Telangana, approximately 160 kilometers from Hyderabad. Situated amidst the Nallamala forest range, the waterfall is surrounded by scenic landscapes and dense vegetation, creating a serene ambiance for visitors.

How to Reach

From Hyderabad, Ethipothala Falls can be reached by road via NH 65. The journey takes approximately 3-4 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Macherla Railway Station, the nearest railway station to the falls, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the entrance.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ethipothala Falls is during the monsoon season, from July to September, when the waterfall is in full spate and the surroundings are lush green. However, visitors should exercise caution during heavy rainfall, as the area may become slippery and unsafe for trekking or sightseeing.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Ethipothala Falls does not have any accommodation options nearby. Visitors can choose to stay in hotels or guesthouses in nearby towns like Nagarjuna Sagar, Macherla, or Nalgonda. As for food, there are a few roadside eateries and stalls near the falls, offering snacks and refreshments. However, visitors are advised to carry their own food and water, especially if planning to spend an extended period near the waterfall.

Nearby Attractions

In addition to Ethipothala Falls, visitors can explore other attractions in the Nagarjuna Sagar area. These include the Nagarjuna Sagar Dam, one of the largest dams in India, and the Nagarjuna Sagar Wildlife Sanctuary, home to a variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can also visit the nearby Nagarjunakonda Island, known for its archaeological sites and Buddhist ruins.

3→ Koulas Fort

Introduction to Koulas Fort

|

About the Place: Koulas Fort is a historic fortress located atop a rocky hill in the Nizamabad district of Telangana. Believed to have been built during the Kakatiya dynasty in the 14th century, the fort served as a strategic stronghold and played a significant role in the region's defense. Surrounded by rugged terrain and steep cliffs, Koulas Fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and is a popular destination for history enthusiasts and adventure seekers. The fort's architectural marvels, including its massive stone walls, bastions, and gateways, reflect its rich heritage and cultural significance.

Geography

Koulas Fort is located near the town of Jukkal in the Nizamabad district of Telangana, approximately 190 kilometers from Hyderabad. Situated atop a rocky hill, the fort overlooks the town and is surrounded by scenic landscapes, making it an ideal destination for trekking and sightseeing.

How to Reach

From Hyderabad, Koulas Fort can be reached by road via NH 44. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours by car or bus. Alternatively, one can take a train to Nizamabad Railway Station, the nearest railway station to the fort, and then hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the entrance.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Koulas Fort is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities like trekking and sightseeing. However, visitors should avoid visiting during the peak summer months (April to June) due to scorching temperatures.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Koulas Fort does not have any accommodation options nearby. Visitors can choose to stay in hotels or guesthouses in nearby towns like Nizamabad or Jukkal. As for food, there are a few eateries and restaurants in the vicinity, serving local delicacies and snacks. However, visitors are advised to carry their own food and water, especially if planning to spend an extended period exploring the fort.

Nearby Attractions

In addition to Koulas Fort, visitors can explore other attractions in the Nizamabad district. These include the Nizamabad Fort, a historic fortress built during the Rashtrakuta dynasty, and the Pocharam Wildlife Sanctuary, home to a variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can also visit the Alisagar Reservoir, a scenic water body surrounded by lush greenery, ideal for picnics and boating.
