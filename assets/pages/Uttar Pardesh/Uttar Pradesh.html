1→ Uttar Pradesh

Introduction to Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh, often overshadowed by its iconic landmarks such as the Taj Mahal and Varanasi, is a state brimming with lesser-known treasures waiting to be explored. Away from the bustling crowds, these offbeat destinations offer a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Let's embark on a journey to discover four such hidden gems of Uttar Pradesh.

Chitrakoot

About the Place: Nestled in the Vindhya Range of Uttar Pradesh, Chitrakoot is a sacred town steeped in mythology and spirituality. Revered as the place where Lord Rama, his wife Sita, and his loyal devotee Lord Hanuman spent their years of exile, Chitrakoot is dotted with ancient temples, serene ghats, and picturesque landscapes. The town holds immense significance for followers of Hinduism and attracts pilgrims seeking solace and blessings.

Geography

Chitrakoot is located approximately 250 kilometers from the capital city of Lucknow and 145 kilometers from Allahabad. Situated along the banks of the Mandakini River, amidst lush green forests and rocky hills, Chitrakoot offers a serene and tranquil ambiance that is perfect for spiritual reflection and exploration.

How to Reach

From Lucknow or Allahabad, Chitrakoot can be reached by road via NH 35. Regular buses and trains operate between these cities and Chitrakoot, providing convenient transportation options for travelers. The journey takes approximately 4-5 hours by road and 2-3 hours by train.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Chitrakoot is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the town's temples and natural beauty. However, visitors should avoid the summer months, from April to June, when temperatures can soar, making outdoor activities uncomfortable.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Chitrakoot offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget, including guesthouses, budget hotels, and ashrams. These accommodations provide comfortable lodging and warm hospitality for pilgrims and travelers. In terms of food, visitors can savor traditional vegetarian cuisine at local eateries and ashrams, with specialties such as kadhi chawal, kachori sabzi, and jalebi being popular choices

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include the Kamadgiri Temple, a sacred hill believed to be the original Chitrakoot mentioned in the Ramayana, and the Hanuman Dhara, a natural spring where Lord Hanuman is said to have appeared. The Gupt Godavari Caves, located on the banks of the Godavari River, are another noteworthy landmark known for their ancient rock-cut chambers and underground tunnels

2→ Dudhwa National Park

Introduction to Dudhwa National Park

About the Place: Dudhwa National Park, located in the Lakhimpur Kheri district of Uttar Pradesh, is a hidden gem for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Spread over an area of approximately 490 square kilometers, the park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species such as the Bengal tiger, Indian rhinoceros, and swamp deer. The park's pristine forests, grasslands, and wetlands offer a unique opportunity to explore the wilderness and observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

Geography

Dudhwa National Park is situated approximately 250 kilometers from Lucknow and 430 kilometers from Delhi. The park is nestled along the Indo-Nepal border, amidst the fertile plains and marshy grasslands of the Terai region. The diverse topography of the park includes dense forests, grassy meadows, and meandering rivers, creating a rich and biodiverse ecosystem.

How to Reach

From Lucknow or Delhi, Dudhwa National Park can be reached by road via NH 730. Regular buses and trains operate between these cities and Lakhimpur Kheri, the nearest town to the park. From Lakhimpur Kheri, visitors can hire local transportation or take a short drive to reach the park's entrance

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Dudhwa National Park is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant, and the park's wildlife is easily spotted near water sources. However, visitors can also explore the park during the summer months, from March to June, to witness the migratory birds and flowering trees in full bloom.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Dudhwa National Park offers limited accommodation options, including forest lodges and eco-resorts operated by the Uttar Pradesh Forest Department. These accommodations provide basic amenities and an immersive experience in nature for travelers. In terms of food, visitors can enjoy meals at the park's canteen or carry their own provisions for picnics and outdoor dining.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include the Sathiana and Sonaripur Grasslands, known for their picturesque landscapes and abundant wildlife sightings. The Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary, situated adjacent to Dudhwa National Park, is another noteworthy landmark that offers opportunities for birdwatching and nature walks.

3→ Bateshwar

Introduction to Bateshwar

About the Place: Bateshwar, located in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh, is a hidden gem known for its ancient temples, serene ghats, and spiritual significance. Spread over an area of approximately 25 square kilometers, Bateshwar is home to more than 100 temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, making it one of the largest temple complexes in North India. The town holds immense religious importance for devotees and pilgrims, who flock to its sacred sites to seek blessings and offer prayers.

Geography

Bateshwar is situated approximately 75 kilometers from Agra and 210 kilometers from Delhi. The town is nestled along the banks of the Yamuna River, amidst fertile plains and verdant orchards. The serene ambiance and scenic beauty of Bateshwar make it a tranquil retreat for spiritual seekers and nature lovers alike.

How to Reach

From Agra or Delhi, Bateshwar can be reached by road via NH 19. Regular buses and trains operate between these cities and Bateshwar, providing convenient transportation options for travelers. The journey takes approximately 2-3 hours by road and 1-2 hours by train.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Bateshwar is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the town's temples and attending religious festivals. However, visitors can also explore Bateshwar during the monsoon season, from July to September, to witness the lush greenery and blooming flowers.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Bateshwar offers limited accommodation options, including guesthouses and budget hotels located near the temple complex. These accommodations provide basic amenities and comfortable lodging for pilgrims and travelers. In terms of food, visitors can enjoy vegetarian cuisine at local eateries and ashrams, with specialties such as chole bhature, paneer tikka, and lassi being popular choices.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include the Chambal Wildlife Sanctuary, known for its diverse flora and fauna, including endangered species such as the gharial and Indian skimmer. The Agra Fort, situated approximately 75 kilometers from Bateshwar, is another noteworthy landmark that offers insights into India's rich architectural and cultural heritage.

4→ Sarnath

Introduction to Sarnath

About the Place: Sarnath, located on the outskirts of Varanasi in the Uttar Pradesh, is a sacred Buddhist site revered as the place where Lord Buddha preached his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. Also known as the "birthplace of Buddhism," Sarnath is home to ancient stupas, monasteries, and archaeological remains that bear testimony to its illustrious past. The town's tranquil ambiance and spiritual significance make it a popular pilgrimage destination for Buddhists and travelers seeking spiritual enlightenment and cultural exploration

Geography

Sarnath is situated approximately 10 kilometers from Varanasi and 300 kilometers from Lucknow. The town is nestled amidst lush greenery and scenic landscapes, with the Ganges River flowing nearby. The serene ambiance and historical significance of Sarnath attract visitors from all over the world, who come to pay homage to Lord Buddha and explore the town's ancient monuments and relics

How to Reach

From Varanasi or Lucknow, Sarnath can be reached by road via NH 31. Regular buses and taxis operate between these cities and Sarnath, providing convenient transportation options for travelers. The journey takes approximately 30-45 minutes by road.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Sarnath is during the winter months, from October to March, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the town's temples and archaeological sites. However, visitors can also explore Sarnath during the monsoon season, from July to September, to witness the lush greenery and blooming flowers.

Nearby Accommodation and Food

Sarnath offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget, including guesthouses, budget hotels, and luxury resorts. These accommodations provide comfortable lodging and warm hospitality for pilgrims and travelers. In terms of food, visitors can enjoy vegetarian cuisine at local eateries and restaurants, with specialties such as thukpa, momos, and Tibetan bread being popular choices.

Nearby Attractions

Nearby attractions include the Dhamek Stupa, one of the oldest and most well-preserved stupas in India, and the Mulagandha Kuti Vihara, a modern Buddhist temple known for its beautiful frescoes and murals. The Sarnath Archaeological Museum, situated near the stupa, houses a rich collection of artifacts and relics dating back to the Mauryan and Gupta periods.
