1→ Rudraprayag

Introduction to Rudraprayag

Named after Lord Shiva's Rudra avatar , Rudraprayag is an ancient holy town situated at the confluence of Alaknanda and Mandakini rivers. While the town is known for temples and pristine natural beauty, the presence of two separate routes for Badrinath (around 150 km away) and Kedarnath Dham (around 50 km away) from Rudraprayag make it an important destination for religious tourism, attracting tourists and devotees throughout the year, who make a beeline for the Shiva and Jagdamba temples here. Rudraprayag is the Holy city named after the Rudra avatar of Lord Shiva.One of the Panch Prayag (five confluences) of the Alaknanda River. This place is surrounded by immense natural beauty,Surrounded by lakes, glaciers and many more sightseeings. Rudraprayag is also surrounded by dense forests and mountains and offer several offbeat paths for calming nature walks.

History

Hindu mythology says that to master the mysteries of music, sage Narad did a severe penance to appease Lord Shiva, who appeared in his Rudra avatar to bless Narad. The Pandava Dance, a dance form based on the dance and music of the Kaurava and Pandavas as described in the epic Mahabharata, is very popular among locals in this district. It is believed that after Kurukshetra war, to repent for the death of kin, the Pandavas visited this region to pray to Lord Shiva. Even today, locals here celebrate Pandav Leela in the memory of that visit.

2→ Kartikswami Temple

Introduction to Kartikswami Temple

Around 40 km from Rudraprayag on the Rudraprayag to Pokhri route is a village Kanak Chauri from where a 3-km trek leads to Kartikswami. This place has a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva’s son Kartikeya. The temple sits in natural beauty and one can have a close and panoramic view of the Himalayan peaks . Perched on a hilltop near the village of Kanakchauri, Rudraprayag, the Kartik Swami temple is one of the most prominent ones dedicated to Lord Shiva's son Lord Kartik, in the state. Situated atop Kronch Parvat, the temple pays homage to Lord Kartik, also known as Lord Muruga or Lord Murugan in parts of India, and his devotion to his parents.

History of the Temple

While there are many legends about this temple, the most popular one dates back to the era when Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati's two sons - Lord Kartik and Lord Ganesha - were asked to circumambulate the universe seven times. Lord Kartik set off on the expedition, undertaking the challenging task of taking seven rounds of the universe, whereas, Lord Ganesha took rounds of his parents, referring to them as his universe. When Lord Kartik returned and learnt of how Lord Ganesha had completed the task and won the appreciation of Lord Shiva, he was enraged. The Puranas say that an angry Lord Kartik left Kailash, his parents' abode, and arrived at Kronch Parvat. Some legends say that after this, Lord Kartik went to a mountain named Srisailam in Southern India, where he was known as Skanda, one of his many names apart from Lord Muruga, Lord Murugan and Lord Subramanya. Others say, Lord Kartik was so furious, he sacrificed his physical self at Kronch Parvat to appease his parents.

How To Reach

The temple sits atop the mountain, silhouetted against the backdrop of snow-covered Himalayan peaks, painting a dreamy image. When it rains, the ambience becomes surreal and it seems the temple is rising from the clouds. A 3-km trek from Kanakchauri village ending with an 80-step-climb leads to the serene temple adorned with thousands of bells. It is believed that offering a bell here on Kartik Purnima can make one's wishes come true. The sandhya aarti or evening prayers are especially enchanting here, with the entire temple premises echoing with tolling bells and hymns.

Sangam

This is the holy confluence of the two rivers. Devotees take a dip here. A flight of stairs lead down to the river, where a cremation ghat is also located. A serene aarti is held here every evening, at 5 pm in winter and at 7 pm in summer. Just above the river bank is the Narad Shila, a dark stone, which folklore says marks the spot where sage Narad had sat in meditation. A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is located here.

3→ Mandakini

Introduction to Mandakini

The river Mandakini, which is the most important river coming down from the slopes of Kedarnath peak, joins the Alaknanda at Rudraprayag. The river actually originates from the springs fed by melting snow of Charabari glacier about one km above Kedarnath temple.

Alaknanda

The origin of this river is the alkapuri glacier and it confluences with Mandakini river at Rudraprayag district.Rudraprayag district is situated on the confluence of these two holy rivers.

Vasuki Ganga

The source of the Vasuki Ganga River is the Vasuki Tal.It is a tributary of the Mandakini River.

Koteshwar Mahadev Temple

Koteshwar Mahadev Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is located approximately 3 km from Rudraprayag city centre. According to legends, this place is considered to be the spot where Lord Shiva stopped for meditation on his way to Kedarnath. According to local mythology the temple has existed since the time of Bhasmasura - a demon who turned to ashes anyone whose head he touched. Lord Shiva went into hiding to escape him, resting in various places before coming to the cave at Koteshwar Mahadev. It is believed Lord Shiva rested here for some time, meditating, before confronting and defeating the demons.

4→ Chakrata Hill Station

Introduction to Chakrata Hill Station

So, we have another yet mesmerizing location to travel in Devbhoomi Uttrakhand. Well sometimes we search for the destinations that are not too much elevated and far from our main lands. Well one of our locations for you is Chakrata hill station.

Overview

Chakrata is a beautiful hill station located in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, India. The hill station is surrounded by beautiful forests of oak and rhododendron, and there are many beautiful trekking trails in the area. Chakrata is also known for its beautiful waterfalls, such as Tiger Falls and Kimona Falls, and for its adventure activities, including rock climbing, rappelling, and trekking.

Location

Chakrata is a cantonment town and also a sub district/tehsil, in Dehradun district[1] in the state of Uttarakhand, India. It lies between the Tons and Yamuna rivers, at an elevation of 2118 m, 98 km from the state capital, Dehradun.

Best Time To Travel

During this time, the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, such as trekking, camping, and sightseeing. During the peak season, visitors can also enjoy local festivals and fairs, which are held in the town and nearby villages.

History Of Chakarata

Chakrata has an interesting history, it goes back to the Colonial times. In 1866, the British Indian Army established this cantonment town. It came to be known as Chakrata after the tribe which lived there. The place played a significant role in army campaigns and was a crucial military base during British rule.

How To Reach

  • By Road: Chakrata is well connected by road, so a road trip is not a bad idea from surrounding cities. Chakarata is nearly 3 hours (87 km)drive from Dehradun{the capital of UTTRAKHAND}. You can also include Mussorie a beautiful hill station in your drive to chakarata. From Mussorie Chakarata is just 86 kms, therefore its a great ided where you can cover both the hill stations.
  • By Air: The nearest airport to Chakrata is Jolly Grant Airport which is 115 km.
  • By Railways: The nearest railway station is the Dehradun railway station at a distance of about 87 km.
  • By Cab:You can hire a cab or take a local bus to reach Chakrata directly from both of the above.

Places to visit in and around Chakrata

  • Deoban:If you like exploring the outdoors and a hike sounds good, Deoban is the perfect place for you. This is one of the best Chakrata places to visit in one day. Look up online and you can find some really nice trekking tour packages for the same.
  • Kanasar:Koti Kanasar is an ancient temple dedicated to lord Shiva situated at Altitude: 2178.8 metres. The Deodar trees here are claimed to be the highest in Asia. Koti Kanasar should certainly be on your list of Chakrata places to visit. The majestic beauty of these ancient giants is a sight to behold, with some trees reaching staggering heights and circumferences that command respect and admiration.
  • Tiger Falls:If you are visiting during summer, don’t skip some waterfalls fun at Tiger Falls, located 20 km away from Chakrata. On a winter day, one can have a picnic here while the sun is out. This is the best place to visit in summer.
  • Lakhmandal:If you like the charm of small villages, explore the Uttarakhand culture, and would like to visit ancient Hindu temples, do visit Lakhamandal. Mark it on your list of Chakrata places to visit with family. Also, There used to be a belief that if you dug anywhere in Lakhamandal, you would find a shilling, but there is now a ban on doing so.
